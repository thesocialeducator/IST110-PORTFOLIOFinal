Jennifer Schuett Cold Case Summary

What role did DNA sampling play in solving the case?
DNA sampling was critical in solving the 1990 assault case of Jennifer Schuett. After extracting a DNA profile from the evidence in 2008, forensic scientists matched it in CODIS, identifying Dennis Earl Bradford, a convicted offender, as the perpetrator​(Crime IST (2)).

How and when was the DNA collected?
The DNA was collected from the crime scene in 1990 but analyzed fully in 2008 using advanced technology​.

What should be done with DNA samples from persons not convicted?
Retaining DNA samples ensures long-term justice, linking potential offenders to crimes even after long intervals. This safeguards the community while enabling critical breakthroughs in cold cases​.

Week 12: Societal Issues Portfolio

Topic: Cybercrime and Juvenile Punishment

Argument Summary
Juveniles should not face the same punishment as adults for cybercrimes due to their developmental differences and higher potential for rehabilitation. However, the severity and nature of the crime must influence the response. While rehabilitation-focused interventions like education and mentorship are more effective for minors, severe cases involving malicious intent or significant harm may require tailored legal responses.

Key Points

1.      Developmental Considerations

○       The adolescent brain, particularly the pre-frontal cortex responsible for impulse control, is still maturing into the mid-20s.

○       Impulsivity and lack of foresight in teenagers suggest a focus on reform rather than punitive action.

○       Rehabilitation through education and mentorship can yield positive behavioral changes.

2.      Severity of the Cybercrime

○       Minor hacking due to curiosity differs from cyberstalking or malware distribution.

○       Severe offenses causing significant harm may warrant stricter measures to ensure accountability.

3.      Refutation of Counterarguments

○       While harsh punishment may seem like a deterrent, research shows it has no direct impact on reducing juvenile recidivism.

○       Rehabilitation addresses underlying developmental issues and reduces repeat offenses more effectively than punishment.

4.      Balanced Approach

○       A mix of accountability and reform is necessary to address juvenile cybercrime effectively, ensuring justice while fostering growth.

References

●       American Academy of Child & Adolescent Psychiatry: Insights on adolescent brain development.

●       National Institute of Mental Health: Potential for learning and adaptation in teens.

●       American Psychological Association: Neuroscience of adolescent behavior.

Week 13 Discussion: Crowdsourcing for Innovation

Prompt Selected:

●       Definition and Basics: Define crowdsourcing and briefly explain how it works.

●       Real-World Applications: Share instances where crowdsourcing has been employed successfully.

Definition and Basics

Crowdsourcing is the process of obtaining ideas, services, or content by soliciting contributions from a large group of people, often through online platforms. It taps into the collective intelligence and diverse perspectives of the "crowd" to solve problems, generate ideas, or accomplish tasks that may be challenging for a single entity. By leveraging technology, organizations can efficiently connect with global contributors who bring unique skills and insights.

Real-World Applications

1.      Wikipedia: A prime example of crowdsourcing, Wikipedia has successfully created an open-source encyclopedia. Contributors worldwide collaborate to share knowledge, edit content, and maintain the accuracy of articles. This model ensures the continuous growth of accessible information.

2.      Citizen Science Projects: Projects like Zooniverse use crowdsourcing to engage citizens in scientific research. For instance, volunteers help classify galaxies or identify animal behavior, providing researchers with invaluable data at scale.

3.      Design Challenges: Companies like LEGO invite users to submit product ideas through platforms like LEGO Ideas. Crowdsourcing enables the company to co-create innovative designs and increase customer engagement.

4.      Crowdfunding: Platforms like Kickstarter and Indiegogo allow individuals to fund creative or business ventures by pooling contributions from supporters. This method democratizes funding and enables projects to come to life with global backing.

Impact on Business and Society

Crowdsourcing has revolutionized the way businesses and communities tackle challenges by:

●       Encouraging diverse perspectives that foster innovation.

●       Enabling cost-effective solutions through community-driven collaboration.

●       Creating opportunities for global participation, breaking geographical and socio-economic barriers.

By integrating crowdsourcing into their operations, businesses and organizations can amplify creativity, address complex problems efficiently, and build lasting relationships with their audience.
